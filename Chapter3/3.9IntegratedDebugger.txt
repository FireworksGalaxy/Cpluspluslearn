Defensive programming is a practice whereby the programmer tries to anticipate all of the ways the software could be misused, either by end-users, 
or by other developers (including the programmer themselves) using the code. 
These misuses can often be detected and then mitigated (e.g. by asking a user who entered bad input to try again).
